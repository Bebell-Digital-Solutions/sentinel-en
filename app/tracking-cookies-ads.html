Tracking, Cookies, Ads

Take control of your online privacy by managing tracking, cookies, and personalized ads.

What this is

This section explains how websites and advertisers track your online activity using cookies and other technologies, and provides actionable steps to minimize this tracking and reduce personalized advertising.

Plain definition

Implementing browser settings and tools to limit how companies collect and use your online behavior data for advertising and profiling.

Why it matters

Extensive online tracking can lead to a loss of privacy, manipulation through targeted advertising, and your data being shared or sold to numerous third parties without your full understanding or consent.

Real risk explanation with concrete examples

When you visit a shopping site, cookies remember what you viewed. Later, you might see ads for those exact products on other websites or social media. This is targeted advertising based on tracking. More concerning, this data can build a detailed profile of your interests, habits, and even vulnerabilities, which can be used for purposes beyond simple advertising. Taking control means limiting who can build these profiles.
